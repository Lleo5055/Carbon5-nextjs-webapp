# BRSR Leadership Indicators: Going Beyond the Basics

The Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR) framework in India has evolved significantly since its introduction. While the Essential Indicators form the foundation of mandatory reporting under [SEBI's BRSR circular](https://www.sebi.gov.in/legal/circulars/may-2021/business-responsibility-and-sustainability-reporting-by-listed-entities_50096.html) for the top 250 listed companies, the BRSR Leadership Indicators represent a voluntary layer of advanced disclosure that separates sustainability frontrunners from the rest. These 42 additional topics across environmental, social, and governance dimensions offer companies a pathway to demonstrate genuine commitment to sustainable practices and stakeholder accountability.

## Understanding BRSR Leadership Indicators

BRSR Leadership Indicators are voluntary disclosures that extend beyond the 98 Essential Indicators required under BRSR Core. They cover 42 advanced ESG topics designed to capture sophisticated sustainability practices that most Indian companies haven't yet standardized. These indicators enable businesses to report on emerging best practices, innovative initiatives, and forward-looking commitments that aren't yet mandatory but are increasingly expected by stakeholders.

### What Makes Leadership Indicators Different from Core Indicators

The Essential Indicators focus on baseline governance, risk management, and operational metrics. Leadership Indicators, by contrast, address deeper strategic questions: How is your organization preparing for climate transition? What does your value chain human rights due diligence look like? Are you measuring product-level environmental impacts? These questions signal maturity and competitive advantage.

### Coverage Across ESG Dimensions

Leadership Indicators span environmental sustainability (including Scope 3 emissions and lifecycle assessments), social responsibility (supply chain labour practices, diversity metrics beyond mandatory thresholds), and governance (board effectiveness, stakeholder engagement mechanisms). This breadth means that whether you're an FMCG company, manufacturer, or service provider, there are relevant Leadership Indicators to demonstrate leadership in your sector.

## Why Companies Should Report BRSR Leadership Indicators

Reporting Leadership Indicators isn't just about transparency - it's a strategic business decision with tangible benefits.

### Meeting Investor Expectations

Institutional investors increasingly scrutinize companies beyond mandatory disclosures. Asset managers and ESG-focused funds expect detailed information on climate risk exposure, supply chain resilience, and human capital development. Companies that report Leadership Indicators demonstrate preparedness for evolving investor due diligence requirements and position themselves as lower-risk investments.

### Improving ESG Ratings and Rankings

Global ESG rating agencies like MSCI, Sustainalytics, and Bloomberg weight voluntary disclosures heavily in their assessments. Our guide to [BRSR reporting](/blog/what-is-brsr-reporting) shows that Leadership Indicators directly address gaps these agencies identify. Better ratings translate to lower cost of capital and improved market reputation.

### Building Supply Chain Credibility

For companies with complex supply chains, Leadership Indicators on human rights due diligence, procurement practices, and vendor sustainability assessments signal genuine governance. This matters enormously when multinational clients or conscious consumers evaluate partnerships. It's particularly critical for exporters working with European buyers subject to the Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD).

## Key BRSR Leadership Indicators for Environmental Performance

Environmental Leadership Indicators represent some of the most material topics for large Indian corporates, particularly as climate regulations tighten.

### Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) and Product-Level Impact

Many companies track emissions at the facility level but miss product-specific environmental impacts. Leadership Indicators encourage disclosure of Life Cycle Assessment data - from raw material extraction through end-of-life management. This is particularly relevant for manufacturers and consumer goods companies where product design directly influences customer environmental footprints.

### Scope 3 Value Chain Emissions and Decarbonization

While some BRSR Core indicators touch on Scope 3 emissions, Leadership Indicators push companies to map and measure across their entire value chain. This includes upstream supplier emissions and downstream customer use phase impacts. Companies that report comprehensive Scope 3 data demonstrate alignment with the GHG Protocol and global climate commitments like net-zero targets.

### Climate Transition Plans and TCFD Alignment

Leadership Indicators require disclosure of detailed transition plans: capital allocation to low-carbon technologies, timelines for facility upgrades, and resilience strategies against climate scenarios. These align closely with Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) recommendations and show investors that sustainability isn't a compliance checkbox but an integrated business strategy.

## Social Leadership Indicators: Human Rights Beyond Compliance

### Supply Chain Human Rights Due Diligence

The mandatory BRSR Core asks about labour practices at direct facilities. Leadership Indicators extend this to upstream and downstream partners: How do you assess human rights risks in your supply chain? What remediation mechanisms exist? Have you conducted third-party audits beyond statutory requirements?

This is essential context as Indian regulations increasingly align with global standards - companies strong on supply chain human rights due diligence are already positioned for emerging compliance requirements like India's potential implementation of human rights due diligence mandates.

## How Leadership Indicators Relate to BRSR Core

It's important to understand the relationship between these two layers. Some BRSR Core KPIs and Leadership Indicators address similar topics but at different depths. For instance, [BRSR Core requirements for top 250 companies](/blog/brsr-core-top-250-companies) shows that both frameworks address energy consumption and emissions. However, Core indicators focus on quantitative disclosure, while Leadership Indicators require strategic context, targets, and transition narratives.

## Practical Implementation for Indian Companies

### Strategic Prioritization

Not all 42 Leadership Indicators apply equally to every business. A renewable energy company might prioritize climate transition indicators, while a consumer goods manufacturer might focus on supply chain human rights and product LCA. Start by assessing materiality and stakeholder expectations in your sector.

### Data Infrastructure Investment

Many Leadership Indicators require data that companies haven't historically tracked - Scope 3 emissions, supply chain audit results, or lifecycle impacts. Implementing robust ESG accounting platforms ensures data reliability and auditability, which investors and ratings agencies increasingly verify.

### Stakeholder Communication

Publishing Leadership Indicators without context misses the opportunity. Pair disclosures with narrative explanation: Why these priorities? What challenges did you encounter? How does this investment create value? Thoughtful communication transforms compliance into stakeholder dialogue.

## FAQ

## What are BRSR Leadership Indicators and are they mandatory?

BRSR Leadership Indicators are 42 voluntary ESG topics that extend beyond the 98 mandatory Essential Indicators. Companies can report on them to demonstrate advanced sustainability practices, but they are not required for compliance.

## Which BRSR Leadership Indicators are most important for investors?

Investors prioritize Leadership Indicators that reveal material risks and opportunities: climate transition plans, Scope 3 emissions, supply chain human rights due diligence, and board diversity metrics. These indicators inform investment decisions and cost of capital assessments.

## How do Leadership Indicators relate to BRSR Core?

Leadership Indicators and BRSR Core sometimes address the same topics but at different depths. Core indicators establish quantitative baselines, while Leadership Indicators require strategic narrative, targets, and forward-looking commitments.

## Should SMEs report BRSR Leadership Indicators voluntarily?

SMEs not subject to mandatory BRSR Core reporting may find selective Leadership Indicators valuable for investor relations, supply chain partnerships, or competitive positioning. However, they should prioritize based on stakeholder materiality rather than reporting everything.

## How does BRSR reporting differ from CSRD or SECR frameworks?

BRSR is India-specific and regulatory-driven for large listed companies, while CSRD (EU), SECR (UK), and CCTS (Canada) are jurisdiction-specific mandatory frameworks. Many large Indian exporters must comply with multiple standards simultaneously.

## Conclusion

BRSR Leadership Indicators represent a critical inflection point in corporate sustainability reporting. They acknowledge that a baseline level of disclosure is no longer sufficient - investors, regulators, and customers expect evidence of genuine environmental stewardship, human rights commitment, and governance sophistication. For Indian companies competing globally, reporting Leadership Indicators isn't just about staying ahead of future regulation; it's about securing capital, talent, and partnerships in an increasingly ESG-conscious marketplace.

The companies that lead today will define the norms of tomorrow. By voluntarily adopting BRSR Leadership Indicators, you're not just complying with current expectations - you're building the credibility and data infrastructure that will make tomorrow's mandatory regulations easier to navigate.
